§ 02 The Whole Craft, In Three Beats

Surface → Deeper → Share.
That's the entire move.

Forget memorising lines. The whole game is one repeatable rhythm: ask a surface question, probe the emotion underneath, share one short reflection back. Run that loop three or four times across the date and she leaves feeling like you actually saw her.

Ask Surface

Start with facts

Grounded, logical, easy to answer. Warms her up.

→
Probe Emotion

Why it matters

How it felt, what drew her to it, why it stuck.

→
Share

Add one thought

Short reflection or relate. Never another question.

The One Rule That Matters

Every deep question must be followed by a short share from you. Question → question → question is interrogation. Question → listen → share is chemistry. Most men get the first two beats right and then ruin it on the third.

§ 04 Preview · The 10 Killers

Three categories.
You're using two of them.

Three families of question quietly kill attraction on first dates. None of them feel wrong while you're saying them — that's the whole problem. The full list, with the exact replacements, is inside:

Interview-Mode

The HR Opener

"So tell me about yourself." "What do you do for fun?" Vague, pre-scripted, signals zero attention. She feels screened.

Neediness

The Score-Sheet Question

"So what do you think of me so far?" "Are you having a good time?" Hands her the verdict. You lose before she answers.

Bad-Calibration

The Wrong-Time Depth

"What's your biggest trauma?" "Why are you still single?" Depth at the wrong moment — feels invasive or judgmental.

The fix is always the same: trade the generic question for a specific observation, or a more curious version of the same question. Specific beats vague every time.
